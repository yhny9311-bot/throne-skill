# Concept Glossary

Use this file for `入学：概念启蒙`. Explain concepts in plain Chinese first, then connect them to landscape architecture research.

## Complex Network

- What it is: A way to represent a system as nodes and edges.
- Landscape use: Ecological patches as nodes and corridors as edges; evaluate connectivity, robustness, and key nodes.
- Common metrics: Degree, betweenness, closeness, modularity, robustness.
- Misuse risk: Graph metrics must correspond to ecological meaning, not just mathematical elegance.

## Superlinear Scaling

- What it is: An indicator grows faster than city size. In log-log regression, the exponent is greater than 1.
- Landscape use: Explain how heat exposure, night activity, innovation, or green-service inequality changes with city population.
- Misuse risk: Scaling relationships do not automatically prove mechanism.

## MGWR

- What it is: Multiscale geographically weighted regression; different variables can operate at different spatial bandwidths.
- Landscape use: Identify spatially varying effects of NDVI, NDBI, POI, or socioeconomic variables on LST or green-space access.
- Misuse risk: Needs spatial diagnostics, multicollinearity checks, and careful interpretation.

## SEM

- What it is: Structural equation modeling for direct and indirect relationships among variables.
- Landscape use: Test mechanism paths such as `urban form -> vegetation -> LST -> heat exposure`.
- Misuse risk: Requires theory-driven paths; fit indices do not prove causality.

## Circuit Theory

- What it is: Treats landscape movement like electrical current through a resistance surface.
- Landscape use: Identify ecological corridors, pinch points, barriers, and flow intensity.
- Misuse risk: Resistance surface weights are often the weakest link and need sensitivity analysis.

## Landscape Genetics

- What it is: Connects genetic differentiation with landscape resistance or connectivity.
- Landscape use: Less common in planning papers, but useful when studying species movement and habitat fragmentation.
- Misuse risk: Requires biological sampling and population genetics expertise.

## Ecosystem Services

- What it is: Benefits humans obtain from ecosystems, such as carbon storage, water yield, cooling, recreation, and habitat.
- Landscape use: Evaluate multifunctional green infrastructure, trade-offs, and planning priorities.
- Misuse risk: Service models depend heavily on input parameters and spatial scale.

## Street-View Semantics

- What it is: Extracting visual elements from street-level images by segmentation or detection.
- Landscape use: Quantify visual greenery, sky view, enclosure, safety, comfort, and aesthetic perception.
- Misuse risk: Sampling season, camera position, and model error can bias conclusions.
